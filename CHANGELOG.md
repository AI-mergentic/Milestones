You are likely referring to Ma'alim fi al-Tariq (Signposts on the Road), written by the Egyptian educator and theorist Sayyid Qutb.

Published in 1964, it is widely considered one of the most influential works in modern Islamist thought. While Qutb began his career as a literary critic and secular educator, his time spent in the United States and his subsequent imprisonment in Egypt led him toward a radicalized worldview that shifted the trajectory of political Islam.
Core Concepts of Milestones

The book serves as a manifesto for a complete "re-orientation" of Islamic society. Its influence stems from several provocative concepts:

    Jahiliyyah (Modern Ignorance): Qutb argued that the entire world—including contemporary Muslim-majority countries—had fallen into a state of Jahiliyyah (a term traditionally used to describe pre-Islamic Arabia). He defined this as any system where humans are governed by human-made laws rather than divine law.

    Hakimiyyah (Sovereignty): A central pillar of the book is that sovereignty belongs solely to God. Therefore, any political system based on popular sovereignty or secularism was, in Qutb’s view, a usurpation of God’s authority.

    The Vanguard: Qutb called for the formation of a "vanguard" (tali’a) of true believers who would separate themselves from the "ignorance" of modern society to eventually lead a total transformation.

    Jihad: He reinterpreted Jihad not just as a defensive struggle, but as an active tool to remove the physical obstacles (political systems and leaders) that prevented people from choosing to submit to God.

Historical Impact and Legacy

The publication of Milestones had immediate and long-lasting consequences:

    State Reaction: The Egyptian government under Gamal Abdel Nasser viewed the book as a direct call for sedition. Qutb was executed in 1966, which cemented his status as a martyr (shahid) for many radical movements.

    Ideological Split: The book created a rift within the Muslim Brotherhood. While some leaders opted for a more gradual, political approach, younger members were galvanized by Qutb’s more militant, revolutionary stance.

    Global Influence: The ideas in Milestones laid the intellectual foundation for various groups, ranging from the Egyptian Islamic Jihad to Al-Qaeda. His "us-versus-them" framework became a staple of extremist rhetoric.

A Note on Context

It is important to distinguish between Qutb’s earlier, more moderate social-justice-focused writings (like Social Justice in Islam) and the more rigid, revolutionary tone of Milestones. The latter was written mostly while he was suffering in prison, which many historians argue contributed to the book's uncompromising and "black-and-white" nature.

The chapter titles in Sayyid Qutb's Milestones (Ma'alim fi al-Tariq) are essential to understanding the book's structure, as they move from a theoretical diagnosis of the world's "ailments" to a practical guide for the "vanguard" he hoped to inspire.

While translations vary slightly depending on the publisher, the standard chapter list is as follows:
The Chapters of Milestones

    The Unique Qur'anic Generation (Discusses why the first generation of Muslims was unique and why that purity was lost.)

    The Nature of the Qur'anic Method (Argues that Islam is a practical movement, not just a theoretical philosophy.)

    The Characteristics of the Islamic Society and the Method of its Formation (Defines what makes a society truly "Islamic" versus "Jahili.")

    Jihad in the Cause of God (One of the most famous chapters; it argues that Jihad is a tool to liberate humanity from the "servitude of men.")

    La Ilaha Illa Allah: The Way of Life of Islam (Explores the legal and social implications of the declaration of faith.)

    The Universal Law (Discusses the harmony between Islamic law and the natural laws of the universe.)

    Islam is the Real Civilization (Argues that civilization is defined by human freedom from materialist and pagan systems.)

    The Islamic Concept and Culture (Discusses how a Muslim should source their knowledge and reject "alien" influences.)

    A Muslim’s Nationality and His Belief (Claims that a Muslim’s true "nationality" is their faith, rather than an ethnic or geographic identity.)

    Far-Reaching Changes (Addresses the need for a total transformation of the world's social and political structures.)

    The Faith Triumphant (A concluding encouragement to the "vanguard" to remain steadfast despite persecution.)

    This is the Way (The final call to action based on the lessons of the previous chapters.)

Key Themes Across Chapters

If you look at the progression, the first few chapters focus on re-establishing the identity of the believer, while the middle chapters focus on defining the enemy (Jahiliyyah) and the mechanism of change (Jihad). The final chapters provide moral and spiritual support for those who choose to follow this path, often drawing parallels between the struggles of the author and the struggles of early Islamic figures.


Categorizing the chapters of Milestones helps clarify Qutb’s transition from a theologian to a political revolutionary. He structured the book to first redefine the Muslim identity, then identify the "enemy," and finally provide a manual for the struggle.

Summary of the Progression

This thematic flow represents a radicalization pipeline.

    Identity: It starts by making the reader feel part of an elite, "pure" group (The Vanguard).

    Conflict: It then creates a sharp binary between that group and the rest of the world, which is labeled as "ignorant" and "un-Islamic," necessitating a physical struggle.

    Endurance: It concludes by framing any hardship or imprisonment resulting from that struggle as a sign of divine favor and historical necessity.

Qutb’s definition of "Nationality" in Chapter 9 differs from modern concepts of the nation-state.

1. The NPC and the Trial
The NPC: Malak al-Haq (The Messenger of Truth)

Malak is not a tribal chief; he is an "Awakener." He is gaunt, his skin tanned to the texture of cracked leather, and his eyes are clouded with cataracts—yet he moves with terrifying precision. He wears robes made from the banners of defeated clans and carries a staff topped with a sun-bleached half-elven skull.

    The Vibe: He speaks in a rasping whisper that carries over the desert wind. He doesn't argue; he simply states the "Truth" as if it were a physical law of nature. He is known for "Takfir"—the act of looking a man in the eye and declaring his soul dead before ordered his execution.

The Trial of the Scoured Earth

Before taking the oath, the initiate is taken to a "Salt Flat of Silence."

    The Task: The initiate must survive three days without water while tethered to a stone pillar.

    The Choice: On the second day, a "moderate" member of their own family (or a captured Half-Elf) is placed nearby with a skin of water. To pass, the initiate must not only refuse the water but must curse the provider and eventually perform a "Severing"—spilling the water into the sand to prove that the thirst for the Law is greater than the thirst of the flesh.

    The Result: If they survive, their old name is buried in the sand. They emerge as a "Vanguard," their throat permanently scarred by the salt, symbolizing they can no longer speak the "soft words" of the old world.

2. The "Crimes against the Dune"

The Vanguard uses these "crimes" to justify their total war against the Liminal Union and the Halfelven. In their eyes, these aren't political disagreements; they are "metaphysical abominations."

    The Crime of Dilution (Blood-Mixing): The existence of Half-Elves is seen as a physical manifestation of Jahiliyyah. They believe that mixing lineages "muddies the spirit" and makes a person incapable of receiving the Fathers' truth.

    The Crime of Usury (Commerce): The Liminal Union’s focus on trade and coin is seen as "worship of the Golden Calf." The Vanguard believes that value only exists in what is earned through struggle or divine decree, not through "cleverness" or "contracts."

    The Crime of Innovation (Bid'ah): The use of arcane magic or technology that simplifies life is a sin. Hardship is the only path to purity; anything that makes life "easy" (like Union aqueducts or Elven enchantments) is a tool of the devil to make men soft and forgetful of the Fathers.

    The Crime of Border-Crossing: The Union’s attempt to bring different peoples under one law is seen as an act of war against the "Natural Order." The Vanguard believes the desert was meant to divide the worthy from the worthless, not to be bridged by roads.

A Description of the Dunefolk:
The Dunefolk are a faction of humans from the deserts and hills of the southern lands. The Dunefolk specialize in using terrain features to coordinate attacks at dawn or dusk. Their ranks boast lawful and liminal units, healers, high-accuracy melee fighters, and fearsome horse-mounted archers. While Dunefolk units tend to be more expensive than those of the Loyalist faction, they make up for this with high mobility — especially on hilly terrains.

An offshoot of a forgotten nomadic civilization, the Dunefolk lay claim to the river valleys and oases of the Sandy Wastes. How they came to inhabit this far corner of the Great Continent is unknown. Their legends tell of many long and perilous travels through far-flung lands, but the true origin of their people is a topic of endless and heated debate among even the most erudite of their scholars.

Whatever their origin, the Dunefolk have thrived. Bustling cities stand proudly in the largest fertile regions. Skilled artisans, fine smiths, and wealthy merchants form the backbone of the urban economy. Each of these cities also enjoys a degree of independence less common in the more centralized nations to the north, many even maintaining their own standing armies. In times of need, however, each and all rally to a higher authority designated to protect the superior interest of the nation.

Those who have not settled in these urban centers still adhere to the lifestyle of their ancestors, roaming the dunes and leading their herds from one watering hole to another. They are most active during the early hours of dawn and the onset of dusk, when the wastelands are neither too hot nor too cold. Their skill at moving through the sands is excellent even by Dunefolk standards. Although sometimes regarded with contempt by their city counterparts, they provide an invaluable service as mobile, light troops in war, or as escorts for trading caravans during times of peace.

The Dunefolk’s inclination towards trade and exploration has allowed their cities to amass immense fortunes, a fact regarded both with admiration and envy by other races. Mutual interests have fostered cordial relations with neighboring Naga tribes, but more secretive races such as Drakes and Elves have always considered Dunefolk expeditions to be too intrusive, especially when they venture close to territorial boundaries. It is not uncommon for caravans to fall prey to troll ambushes in the mountains, something that has given rise to countless tales of unimaginable treasure amassed in hidden caves.

As a result of living in hostile environments for centuries, the Dunefolk have developed rational methods of enquiry through which they continue to improve their understanding of the world. Their study of herbal medicine keeps their warriors and workers fresh and healthy. Their knowledge of alchemy allows them to tame fire and wield it as a deadly weapon in battle. At the same time, this analytical mindset has distanced them from magical arts; to the rational mind, magic is uncontrollable, unpredictable, and hence unreliable. For this reason, the Dunefolk especially loathe the perversions of necromancy and the dark arts, even more so than other races.

The Dunefolk’s inquisitive and explorative nature does not preclude military strength. Not only do they field nimble light troops, cataphracts, and heavily armored infantry, but their keen knowledge of technology grants them a decisive advantage over their opponents. When facing the Dunefolk in battle, however, the most fearsome sight is certainly their deployment of ferocious and bizarre beasts. From the majestic Roc to the imposing Wyvern, their synergy with these creatures allows for great versatility in combat. The origins of this tradition likely lie in the heritage of the Dunefolk’s distant past in the exotic far corners of Irdya.

    * In summary, tactics and units that emphasize impact damage, magic attacks, high damage output, and mobility to pressure healing and terrain disadvantages work effectively against the Dunefolk faction in Wesnoth.

To integrate the heavy ideological themes of Sayyid Qutb’s Milestones into a Battle for Wesnoth campaign, we will frame the Dunefolk as the "Vanguard" (the Tali’a) and a custom Liminal Union as the embodiment of Jahiliyyah (the "Ignorant" world).

In this scenario, the Dunefolk are not just desert mercenaries; they are a radicalized faction seeking to dismantle the "impure" alliances of Wesnoth.
Campaign Map: The Path of the Scourge
Chapter 1: The Unique Qur'anic Generation

    Abstract: Qutb argues the first generation of Muslims was the purest because they derived their entire identity from the Quran alone, unpolluted by outside cultures.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Primal Tribe. The player starts with a small, elite group of Dunefolk units. You must win a skirmish against "corrupt" desert cousins using only Level 1 units, representing the return to "primitive" excellence.

Chapter 2: The Nature of the Qur'anic Method

    Abstract: Belief is not a theory but a movement. The "Method" is a stage-by-stage process of action.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Movement Begins. A mission where you cannot hold villages for gold. Instead, you gain gold only by defeating enemies, forcing a "dynamic movement" playstyle rather than a static defense.

Chapter 3: The Characteristics of the Islamic Society

    Abstract: Society is defined by submission to God. Anything else—nationalism, tribalism, or democracy—is "Ignorance."

    Wesnoth Integration: The Great Severing. You encounter a Dunefolk sub-faction allied with the Liminal Union. You must declare them Takfir (apostates) and eliminate their leader to "purify" the desert society.

Chapter 4: Jihad in the Cause of God

    Abstract: Jihad is a proactive tool to remove physical obstacles (governments/armies) that prevent the establishment of God's sovereignty.

    Wesnoth Integration: Sunder the Union. A siege scenario. The Liminal Union (a mix of Humans and Elves) has built a fortress on a sacred oasis. You must destroy the fortress to "liberate" the land from man-made laws.

Chapter 5: La Ilaha Illa Allah (The Way of Life)

    Abstract: The declaration of faith is a total political and social system, rejecting all human-made authority (Hakimiyyah).

    Wesnoth Integration: The Law of the Sands. A survival map where your units gain a "Zeal" buff but lose the ability to heal at neutral villages, relying entirely on "Vanguard Medics" (custom Dunefolk herbalists).

Chapter 6: The Universal Law

    Abstract: Divine law is in harmony with the laws of the universe (nature).

    Wesnoth Integration: The Desert's Will. A night-time mission. The "Liminal Union" suffers heavy penalties from sandstorms, while your units gain movement bonuses, showing that the desert itself obeys the Vanguard’s law.

Chapter 7: Islam is the Real Civilization

    Abstract: Civilization is not defined by technology or art, but by the liberation of man from serving other men.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Ash of Libraries. You must burn a Union archive. The goal is to prove that their "knowledge" is a shackle, and the only true civilization is the primitive obedience of the Dune-kin.

Chapter 8: The Islamic Concept and Culture

    Abstract: A Muslim should only take knowledge from divine sources, rejecting Western/Foreign philosophies.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Forbidden Lore. You face an enemy mage from the Liminal Union. You are forbidden from using any magical items dropped by the enemy; you must destroy them for "Purity Points" instead.

Chapter 9: A Muslim’s Nationality and His Belief

    Abstract: A believer's only "country" is the land where the Law is implemented. Blood and soil are irrelevant.

    Wesnoth Integration: Blood of the Covenant. You must execute a Dunefolk "Prince" (blood relative) who tried to make peace with the Union, proving that the Creed is thicker than the blood of the father.

Chapter 10: Far-Reaching Changes

    Abstract: The world is so corrupt that only a total, revolutionary change can save it.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Great Reset. A massive battle where the map changes mid-way (a cataclysmic sandstorm levels the Union's stone walls), shifting the terrain to favor nomadic units.

Chapter 11: The Faith Triumphant

    Abstract: Victory is spiritual. Even if the Vanguard is killed, their martyrdom ensures the eventual triumph of the Law.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Martyr’s Stand. A "Defeat is Victory" scenario. You must hold a pass against overwhelming Union forces. The mission ends when your leader dies, but the "Success" screen triggers because your sacrifice inspired a global uprising.

Chapter 12: This is the Way

    Abstract: A final warning that the path is long and painful, but it is the only road to salvation.

    Wesnoth Integration: The Narrow Horizon. The final mission. You lead a handful of "Chosen" units into the heart of the Union capital. No reinforcements. Only the "Way" remains.

New Faction Keyword: Hakimiyyah

In this Wesnoth mod, Dunefolk units with the Hakimiyyah trait do not pay upkeep while they are on desert tiles, representing their total reliance on the "Natural/Divine" order rather than a state-run economy.
